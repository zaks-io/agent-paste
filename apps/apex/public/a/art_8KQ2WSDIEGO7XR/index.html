A weekend in San Diego

What are some good weekend things to do in San Diego? Here is a short, walkable plan that mixes the park, the coast, and one long dinner. No car-dependent detours, no tourist traps you'll regret.

two days · spring or fall is best · budget for one nice meal

Where to go

01

Balboa Park

1,200 acres of gardens, Spanish-revival architecture, and a dozen museums. Start at the Botanical Building, wander the Prado, and end at the cactus garden. Free to walk; museums are individually ticketed.

morning · free to enter

02

La Jolla Cove

Sea lions, tide pools, and some of the clearest water on the coast. Walk the cliff path from the Cove to Children's Pool, then grab a coffee on Girard Avenue. Go early to beat both the crowds and the smell.

midday · bring water shoes

03

Sunset Cliffs

The best free sunset in the city. Park along Ladera Street, walk south, and find a flat rock. Stay back from the edge, the sandstone crumbles. Time it for about thirty minutes before sundown.

evening · arrive before golden hour

04

Little Italy

The densest, most walkable food neighborhood downtown. The Saturday Mercato farmers market is worth planning around. Otherwise: pasta on India Street, a negroni after, gelato on the walk back.

dinner · reservations help on weekends

A sample two days

  • Sat AMBalboa Park: Botanical Building, the Prado, one museum.
  • Sat PMLa Jolla Cove walk, then coffee on Girard.
  • Sat eveSunset Cliffs for the sunset, dinner in Little Italy.
  • Sun AMCoronado: ferry over, beach walk, breakfast by the Hotel del.
  • Sun PMOld Town for history and tacos before you head out.
